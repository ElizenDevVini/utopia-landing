How it works

  1. 1

    Buy a block

    Every plot in utopia is a deed. One block, one owner, recorded on chain.

  2. 2

    The land works

    Each plot is backed by a basket of tokenized stocks. When the basket earns, the land earns.

  3. 3

    Yield stays with the deed

    Hold the block and collect the APY. Sell it, and the yield goes with it.

Every block bought is a block built. Deeds mint as blocks. The skyline is the ledger of who owns what.

Built on Robinhood Chain

Robinhood Chain is Robinhood's Arbitrum-based network for tokenized stocks. The rails that put equities on chain are the rails that back the land. It went live in 2026, and utopia runs on its testnet today: the grid below is real contract state.

The land, live

This is the real grid, read from Robinhood Chain's testnet. 1,024 plots on a verified contract, priced in UTOP, the city's token. Deeds stream testnet stock tokens until you claim them, and as UTOP's market grows, prices and yield scale with it. To buy — and for your land, yield, and the market — open the dashboard.

Questions

Is any of this live?
On testnet, yes. The grid above is read from Robinhood Chain's testnet. The plots, the deeds, and the yield are real contracts moving tokens that cost nothing and are worth nothing.
How do I try it?
Get test ETH for gas from the chain faucet, then 1,000 UTOP from the button on the dashboard. Click an empty plot, buy it, come back later and claim what it streamed.
What backs the yield?
A treasury of testnet stock tokens held by the land contract. Each plot streams one stock at a fixed base rate, scaled by the UTOP market multiplier: when the token's market grows, every deed streams more. Finite, visible on the explorer, no magic.
Why Robinhood Chain?
Because that is where the tokenized stocks live. Building the land on the rails that hold the equities keeps the whole loop on one chain.

utopia runs on Robinhood Chain's testnet. The blocks are real; the money is not. Test ETH and testnet stock tokens have no value. Not an offer of anything.
